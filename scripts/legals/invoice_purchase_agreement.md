### 1. Sale and Purchase

4.1 Sale. The Seller sells, assigns, and transfers absolutely to the Purchaser all its present and future right, title, and interest in and to each Assigned Receivable and Related Rights on each Cut-off Date.

4.2 Perfection. The Parties intend the assignment to be perfected at law. The Seller shall:
	•	deliver digital invoices and metadata;
	•	issue corresponding Receivable Tokens to the Purchaser’s wallet;
	•	execute a notice of assignment to each Payer; and
	•	assist the Purchaser to register a financing statement on the PPSR (if required for perfection by control).

4.3 No Recourse. Except for breaches of warranty or repurchase obligations, the Purchaser has no recourse to the Seller for non-payment or credit loss of a Payer.

4.4 Title and Risk. Legal and beneficial ownership transfers on the Cut-off Date; credit and dilution risk passes concurrently.

⸻

5. Purchase Price and Settlement

5.1 Purchase Price. Calculated per Schedule 2. Payment is made by tokenised or fiat transfer through the Platform on the Settlement Date.

5.2 Adjustments. Purchase Price may be adjusted for early payment discounts, credit notes, or dilutions occurring post-sale.

5.3 Holdback / Reserve. The Purchaser may retain a percentage as a Dilution Reserve or First-Loss Holdback.

5.4 True Sale Confirmation. The Seller acknowledges that the consideration paid constitutes full value and that title passes notwithstanding any servicing or residual interest retained.

⸻

6. Eligibility and Offer Process
	•	Receivables must meet Eligibility Criteria in Schedule 1 (credit quality, currency, aged ≤ 90 days, enforceable, undisputed, GST-compliant).
	•	The Seller uploads invoice data to the Platform; the Purchaser confirms acceptance by digital signature or smart-contract confirmation.
	•	Each accepted invoice is listed in a Purchase Schedule and linked to a corresponding Receivable Token.

7. Representations and Warranties

The Seller represents, as at each Cut-off Date:
	1.	Receivables are valid, enforceable, unconditional and not subject to set-off or dispute.
	2.	The Seller has good title, free of Encumbrance.
	3.	Each invoice complies with the A New Tax System (Goods and Services Tax) Act 1999 (Cth).
	4.	No double assignment, securitisation, or digital representation exists elsewhere.
	5.	All data provided to the Platform is true, complete, and accurate.
	6.	Seller and Payers are not sanctioned entities and comply with AML/CTF laws.
	7.	The Seller is solvent and not under any insolvency process.

⸻

8. Covenants
	•	Maintain accurate records for 7 years; allow audit and data access.
	•	Not amend, waive or cancel any Purchased Receivable without Purchaser consent.
	•	Promptly forward Collections to the Collections Account.
	•	Comply with Servicing Standards and provide monthly performance data.
	•	Co-operate in PPSR filings, payer notices, and verification statements.
	•	Provide ongoing KYC and sanctions updates.

⸻

9. Servicing

9.1 Appointment. The Seller acts as Servicer until termination or replacement.
9.2 Duties. Collect and apply payments, manage disputes, and report performance via the Platform within T + 2 days.
9.3 Remuneration. Servicing Fee as specified in Schedule 3, net of recoveries.
9.4 Termination. Upon Event of Default, insolvency, or breach, the Purchaser may appoint a Back-Up Servicer.
9.5 Data Escrow. Seller shall maintain replicated data (off-chain and IPFS metadata) accessible to Purchaser on default.

⸻

10. Events of Default

Trigger events include:
	1.	Payment default by Seller or failure to remit Collections.
	2.	Breach of warranty or covenant not cured within 5 Business Days.
	3.	Insolvency, administration, or winding-up of Seller.
	4.	Unauthorised duplication or transfer of Receivable Tokens.
	5.	Regulatory non-compliance or sanctions listing.

Remedies: suspension of further purchases, repurchase of affected receivables, set-off against future settlements, or termination.

⸻

11. Repurchase Obligations

Upon breach or ineligibility, the Seller must repurchase the affected Receivable within 3 Business Days at Outstanding Balance + accrued discount − collections received.

⸻

12. True Sale, Non-petition, and Insolvency Protections

12.1 The Parties intend each sale to be an absolute assignment and true sale.
12.2 The Seller acknowledges that Purchaser acts as trustee; the Seller has no right or recourse against Trust assets beyond agreed repurchases.
12.3 The Seller undertakes non-petition against the Trustee or any Trust.

Schedule 1 – Eligibility Criteria
	1.	Obligor (Payer) incorporated in Australia and not insolvent.
	2.	Receivable denominated in AUD.
	3.	Payment due ≤ 120 days.
	4.	No dispute or offset.
	5.	Underlying goods/services delivered.
	6.	Minimum credit score / rating per platform matrix.
	7.	GST-compliant tax invoice issued.
	8.	Not pledged, assigned, or tokenised elsewhere.
	9.	Digital representation conforms to platform data schema.

\text{Purchase Price} = \sum_i \big(\text{Invoice Face Value}_i - \text{Discount}_i - \text{Holdback}_i\big)
