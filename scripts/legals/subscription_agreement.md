### 1. Subscription

⸻

2. Background

A. The Trustee has established the Invoice Collateral Trust (“Trust”) pursuant to a trust deed dated [●] (“Trust Deed”).
B. The Trustee is offering units in the Trust (“Units”), represented digitally as tokenised trust interests recorded on the YieldFabric Platform.
C. The Subscriber wishes to apply for Units as a wholesale investor within the meaning of sections 708(8) and 761G of the Corporations Act 2001 (Cth).
D. The Trustee agrees to issue Units upon acceptance of this Agreement and receipt of cleared subscription funds.

⸻

3. Subscription and Allotment

3.1 Subscription: The Subscriber applies for the number of Units specified in the Subscription Form at a price of AUD 1.00 per Unit (or as otherwise notified).

3.2 Payment:
(a) Payment must be made in cleared funds to the designated Trust Subscription Account or, where permitted, via stablecoin transfer (e.g. A$DC, USDC).
(b) Each payment must reference the Subscriber’s registered wallet or identifier.

3.3 Allotment: Upon acceptance and receipt of funds, the Trustee will:
	•	issue corresponding digital Units via the Platform to the Subscriber’s Whitelisted Wallet;
	•	record the issue on the Register of Unitholders; and
	•	provide a digital confirmation via the Platform.

3.4 Rejection: The Trustee may reject any subscription (in whole or part) at its absolute discretion. Funds will be refunded without interest.

⸻

4. Representations and Warranties of Subscriber

The Subscriber represents and warrants that:
	1.	Wholesale Status: It is a wholesale client and provides satisfactory evidence of that status (e.g., accountant’s certificate, professional investor statement).
	2.	AML/CTF Compliance: It has provided all KYC documents required under the Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorism Financing Act 2006 (Cth) and consents to verification and screening.
	3.	Authority: It has full legal capacity and authorisation to enter this Agreement.
	4.	No Misleading Information: All information supplied is accurate, complete, and not misleading.
	5.	Risk Acknowledgment: It understands the risks of investing in tokenised trust units, including potential loss of capital, illiquidity, and technology risk.
	6.	Independent Advice: It has obtained (or had the opportunity to obtain) independent legal, financial, and tax advice.
	7.	Beneficial Ownership: It subscribes for Units beneficially and not as nominee for a retail investor.
	8.	No Distribution to Retail Clients: It will not resell or transfer Units to any person who is not a wholesale investor.
	9.	Wallet Control: It has exclusive control of the whitelisted wallet receiving Units and will maintain appropriate key security.
	10.	Tax Residency: It has disclosed its tax residency and acknowledges applicable withholding requirements.

	•	distributions may be made in fiat or stablecoin and automatically reinvested as additional Units if elected;

	•	investment performance is not guaranteed by the Trustee, Manager, or any affiliate.

⸻

6. Issue of Units and Register

6.1 The issue of Units is complete when recorded on the on-chain register, which constitutes conclusive evidence of ownership.

6.2 The Platform maintains the canonical record of ownership.
Where discrepancies arise between the on-chain and off-chain registers, the on-chain register prevails after Trustee verification.

6.3 Units are fungible and indivisible. Fractional units may be issued or cancelled via smart-contract rounding.

6.4 The Subscriber consents to the use of digital execution and smart-contract issuance as legally binding under the Electronic Transactions Act 1999 (Cth).

⸻

7. Transfer and Redemption Restrictions

7.1 Units may be transferred only:
	•	to another Whitelisted Wallet approved by the Trustee;
	•	where the transferee satisfies wholesale investor criteria; and
	•	in accordance with the Platform’s operational rules.

7.2 The Trustee may refuse to register any transfer that would breach the law, AML/CTF rules, or Trust Deed.

7.3 Redemptions are subject to available liquidity and any notice period specified in the Information Memorandum.

7.4 The Trustee may suspend redemptions during extraordinary circumstances (e.g., platform failure, market disruption, or regulatory intervention).

⸻

8. AML/CTF, Sanctions, and Compliance

8.1 The Subscriber consents to the Trustee and Manager performing AML/CTF checks, including:
	•	identity verification, beneficial ownership screening, and ongoing monitoring;
	•	sanctions and politically exposed person (PEP) checks;
	•	blockchain analytics to detect suspicious wallet activity.

8.2 The Trustee or Manager may freeze, burn, or re-issue tokens if required by law or regulatory direction.

8.3 The Trustee may disclose relevant information to AUSTRAC or law enforcement without prior notice to the Subscriber.

⸻

9. Taxation and Reporting

9.1 The Subscriber acknowledges that:
	•	Distributions are assessable as trust income in the Subscriber’s hands.
	•	Non-resident investors may be subject to Managed Investment Trust (MIT) or withholding tax.
	•	The Trustee may withhold and remit tax in accordance with applicable law.
	•	The Subscriber must provide a TFN/ABN or withholding may apply.

9.2 The Trustee will issue annual tax statements electronically via the Platform.

⸻

10.3 The Subscriber consents to:
	•	storage and processing of data on servers in Australia and approved international locations;
	•	immutable record-keeping of transactional data;
	•	compliance disclosures to regulators.
