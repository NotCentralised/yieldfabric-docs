COLLATERAL SECURITY DEED

(Enhanced Tokenised Securitisation Version — Acacia Trust Framework)

⸻

1. Parties
	1.	Grantor: [AMAL Trustees Pty Ltd] (ABN [●]) in its capacity as trustee of the Invoice Collateral Trust (the “Grantor” or “Borrower”).
	2.	Secured Party: [Perpetual Corporate Trust Limited] (ABN [●]) as Security Trustee, acting on behalf of the Lender(s) named in the Credit Facility Deed Poll (the “Secured Party”).
	3.	Investment Manager: NotCentralised Pty Ltd (ABN [●]) (the “Manager”) — for operational implementation of control measures via the YieldFabric Platform.

**Secured Parties:** Holders of `investment_token` NFTs ("Investors").

Capitalised terms used in this Deed and not otherwise defined have the meanings given in the Common Definitions Schedule.

⸻

2. Background

A. The Grantor has entered into a Credit Facility Deed Poll and related agreements whereby the Secured Party (for the benefit of the Lenders) provides financial accommodation to the Trust.
B. The Grantor agrees to grant a security interest over certain assets, including tokenised receivables, digital wallets, and bank accounts, to secure all present and future obligations.
C. The Parties intend the security to be perfected under the PPSA and, where applicable, through digital control over tokenised assets on the YieldFabric Platform.

⸻

3. Definitions and Interpretation

3.1 In this Deed, unless otherwise stated:
	•	Collateral means the property described in clause 5.
	•	Digital Assets include any token, stablecoin, or cryptographic key representing ownership or value on a blockchain or distributed ledger.
	•	PPSA means the Personal Property Securities Act 2009 (Cth).
	•	Control, Financing Statement, Security Interest, Attachment, and Perfection have the meanings given in the PPSA.
	•	Platform means the YieldFabric system and any approved successor environment.
	•	Trust Assets means all property of the Invoice Collateral Trust.

3.2 The Electronic Transactions Act 1999 (Cth) applies to all electronic execution, notices, and control of digital assets.

⸻

4. Grant of Security Interest

4.1 The Grantor grants and assigns to the Secured Party a first-ranking security interest (present and future) over all Collateral to secure payment and performance of the Secured Obligations, including all obligations under:
	•	the Credit Facility Deed Poll;
	•	the Investment Management Agreement;
	•	this Deed; and
	•	any ancillary transaction document.

4.2 The security is a fixed charge over Collateral (except where PPSA requires a security interest) and attaches immediately upon acquisition.

4.3 The Secured Party acknowledges that the security is limited to the assets of the Trust, and the Trustee incurs no personal liability beyond those assets.

⸻

5. Collateral Description

The Collateral includes, without limitation:

(a) Receivables and Related Rights
	•	all trade receivables and related rights purchased by the Trust;
	•	all Receivable Tokens representing such receivables;
	•	all proceeds of receivables, including collections, insurance, and recoveries.

(b) Accounts and Cash
	•	all bank, custody, and stablecoin accounts maintained for the Trust, including:
	•	Collections Account,
	•	Distribution Account, and
	•	Reserve Account;
	•	all money standing to the credit of these accounts.

(c) Digital Assets
	•	all tokens issued or held by or on behalf of the Grantor, including:
	•	Receivable Tokens,
	•	Unit Tokens (trust interests),
	•	Stablecoins held as liquidity or reserves,
	•	any other digital assets recorded on the Platform.

(d) Platform and Control Rights
	•	all Platform accounts, API credentials, and access rights enabling the Trustee or Manager to control Receivable Tokens and wallets;
	•	all related operational data and metadata (e.g., IPFS-linked documents and hash records).

(e) Intangible Rights
	•	all rights under the Investment Management Agreement, Servicing Agreements, and IPAs;
	•	all general intangibles connected to the operation or enforcement of receivables;
	•	all claims, proceeds, and insurances relating to the Collateral.

⸻

6. Perfection and Control

6.1 The Parties agree that the Secured Party shall perfect its interest by:
	•	PPSR registration describing the Collateral as “All Present and After-Acquired Property (except where otherwise limited)”;
	•	Control over the Digital Assets under PPSA sections 25–27 (as adapted for tokenised assets);
	•	Account Control Agreements (ACAs) with relevant ADIs and stablecoin custodians; and
	•	Platform-level control over the multi-signature wallets containing Receivable Tokens.

6.2 The Secured Party’s control over wallets is established when:
	•	the Security Trustee is added as a mandatory co-signer on the relevant multi-sig wallet; and
	•	no transfer of Digital Assets can occur without its authorisation.

6.3 The Manager and Trustee must not modify, transfer, or rename wallet keys without Security Trustee consent.

⸻

7. Representations and Warranties

The Grantor represents and warrants that:
	1.	It has full power and authority to enter into this Deed.
	2.	All Trust Assets are owned legally and beneficially by the Trustee as trustee for the Trust.
	3.	The Collateral is free of all other Encumbrances except Permitted Security Interests.
	4.	The grant of security does not breach any law or contractual obligation.
	5.	The Collateral is accurately reflected in the Platform records and reconciled daily.
	6.	All Receivable Tokens and wallet addresses are correctly registered and verified.
	7.	The Trustee is solvent and not under administration.

These representations are repeated on each Drawdown Date.

⸻

8. Undertakings

The Grantor covenants that it shall:
	1.	Maintain the Trust in good standing.
	2.	Maintain accurate and verifiable records of Collateral.
	3.	Immediately notify the Security Trustee of any Event of Default or adverse claim.
	4.	Keep the Collateral fully insured against loss or theft, including cyber and key-loss cover.
	5.	Refrain from creating or allowing any other security interest over the Collateral.
	6.	Not alter wallet or platform configurations without consent.
	7.	Provide monthly reconciliations of receivables, collections, and token balances.
	8.	Maintain PPSR registrations and ensure continuous perfection of security.

⸻

9. Events of Default

An Event of Default occurs if:
	1.	The Grantor defaults under the Credit Facility Deed Poll or this Deed.
	2.	Any representation or warranty is false or misleading.
	3.	The Trustee becomes insolvent or ceases to carry on business.
	4.	There is unauthorised disposal or duplication of Receivable Tokens.
	5.	Collateral is transferred outside approved wallets or jurisdictions.
	6.	Regulatory or sanctions breaches occur.
	7.	There is material deterioration in portfolio performance or asset coverage.

Upon Default, the Security Trustee may enforce its security as provided in clause 10.

⸻

10. Enforcement and Realisation

10.1 Upon an Event of Default, the Security Trustee may:
	•	declare all Secured Obligations immediately due and payable;
	•	take possession or control of all Collateral;
	•	operate the Platform accounts and wallets;
	•	appoint a receiver or replacement trustee;
	•	sell or liquidate Collateral (including through tokenised auction or private sale); and
	•	apply proceeds in accordance with the Enforcement Waterfall (clause 11).

10.2 Each on-chain enforcement transaction shall be verifiable via its transaction hash, recorded in the Enforcement Ledger maintained by the Platform.

⸻

11. Enforcement Waterfall

Proceeds of enforcement shall be applied in the following order:
	1.	Enforcement costs and expenses of the Security Trustee.
	2.	Payment of accrued interest, fees, and principal to the Lender(s).
	3.	Payment of any other secured obligations.
	4.	Residual balance (if any) returned to the Grantor’s Trust Account.

All enforcement events must be reported to investors within five (5) Business Days.

⸻

12. Valuation and Disposal of Collateral

12.1 Collateral may be sold:
	•	by private sale, auction (including on-chain), or public tender;
	•	in parcels or as a whole;
	•	for cash, stablecoin, or other consideration deemed appropriate by the Security Trustee.

12.2 No purchaser need inquire into the authority of the Security Trustee to sell.

12.3 The Security Trustee may credit bid for Collateral on behalf of the Lenders.

⸻

13. Application of PPSA and Notices

13.1 The PPSA applies to this Deed and the security interest created hereby.

13.2 The Grantor waives:
	•	the right to receive verification statements (s157);
	•	any requirement for notice under ss120, 121(4), 125, 129(2), 130, 132(3)(d), 132(4), 135, and 157 of the PPSA.

13.3 The Security Trustee may register financing statements and amendments as necessary.

⸻

14. Digital Settlement and Finality

14.1 All enforcement, repayment, and release transactions executed on the Platform are final and binding once included in a validated block of the canonical blockchain.

14.2 Each on-chain event is cryptographically linked to this Deed via its hash reference.

14.3 Manual or off-chain settlement may occur only in Failover Mode, as defined in the Swap & Token Operation Terms (Annex), and must be reconciled to on-chain records once systems are restored.

⸻

15. Release and Discharge

15.1 Upon full satisfaction of all Secured Obligations, the Security Trustee shall:
	•	execute a Release of Security Interest;
	•	lodge necessary PPSR discharges;
	•	remove its multi-signature control over wallets; and
	•	issue an on-chain “Release Event” recorded via smart contract and audit trail.

15.2 Any partial release must be documented and hashed to the Platform’s Legal Registry.

⸻

16. Subordination and Intercreditor

16.1 The Security Trustee may enter into Intercreditor Arrangements with other secured parties.

16.2 Priority of claims is governed by:
	•	contractual intercreditor terms;
	•	PPSA priority rules; and
	•	record of on-chain seniority timestamps.

16.3 The Security Trustee may hold different classes of security for multiple facilities within the Acacia Trust structure.

⸻

17. Confidentiality and Privacy

Each Party must:
	•	maintain confidentiality of all Collateral and transaction data;
	•	comply with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Trust’s Privacy Notice;
	•	ensure data transfers and blockchain disclosures are pseudonymised wherever possible.

⸻

18. Indemnities

The Grantor indemnifies the Security Trustee and each Lender for all loss or expense arising from:
	•	any default or breach under this Deed;
	•	enforcement proceedings;
	•	legal and advisory costs; and
	•	failure to maintain PPSR perfection or control over Collateral.

This indemnity survives discharge of the security.

19. Notices

Notices may be given by:

Notices are effective upon blockchain confirmation or the next Business Day after email transmission.
