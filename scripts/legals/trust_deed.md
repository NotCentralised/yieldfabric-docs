TRUST DEED

(Enhanced Tokenised Securitisation Version — Acacia Trust Framework)

⸻

**Trust:** YieldFabric Structured Receivables Trust ("Trust")

Capitalised terms used in this Deed and not otherwise defined have the meanings given in the Common Definitions Schedule.

### 1. Establishment of Trust

1.1 Settlor: [Settlor Name] (the “Settlor”) hereby declares that it has paid to [AMAL Trustees Pty Ltd] (ABN [●]) (the “Trustee”) the sum of ten Australian dollars (AUD 10.00) to constitute the initial trust fund (the “Settled Sum”).

1.2 Trust Establishment: The Trustee acknowledges receipt of the Settled Sum and declares that it holds the same and all other property subsequently acquired on trust for the purposes set out in this Deed.

1.3 Name: The Trust is known as the Invoice Collateral Trust (“Trust”), and may include sub-trusts designated by the Trustee for separate asset pools or digital classes.

1.4 Governing Law: This Trust Deed is governed by the laws of New South Wales, Australia, and the Trustee submits to the exclusive jurisdiction of its courts.

⸻

2. Definitions and Interpretation

2.1 Definitions:
Key terms are as defined in Schedule 1 and, where applicable, incorporate definitions from:
	•	the Corporations Act 2001 (Cth);
	•	the Personal Property Securities Act 2009 (Cth); and
	•	the Electronic Transactions Act 1999 (Cth).

2.2 Interpretation:
	•	References to “Units” include their digital representations on the authorised blockchain.
	•	The singular includes the plural and vice versa.
	•	A reference to “Platform” means the YieldFabric Platform or any approved successor.

⸻

3. Purpose of the Trust

3.1 The purpose of the Trust is to:
	•	acquire, hold, and manage receivables, receivable tokens, cash, and other permitted investments;
	•	issue Units to investors representing beneficial interests;
	•	distribute income and capital in accordance with this Deed; and
	•	facilitate tokenised securitisation operations consistent with applicable law.

3.2 The Trust may form part of a broader structure comprising:
	•	the Structured Issuer Trust (aggregator of yields); and
	•	the AUD YieldFabric Cash Trust (liquidity and cash management).

Each operates independently with legally segregated assets and liabilities.

⸻

4. Trustee Appointment and Powers

4.1 Appointment: The Trustee is appointed under this Deed and accepts office as legal owner of the Trust assets.

4.2 General Powers: The Trustee may do all things necessary for the administration of the Trust, including:
	•	acquiring assets under Invoice Purchase Agreements;
	•	entering into Investment Management, Servicing, and Custody Agreements;
	•	opening and operating bank or stablecoin accounts;
	•	registering, minting, or redeeming digital tokens;
	•	engaging advisors, custodians, and auditors; and
	•	executing smart contracts and digital instruments.

4.3 Delegation: The Trustee may delegate powers to the Investment Manager, custodian, or other agents but remains ultimately responsible.

4.4 Reliance: The Trustee may rely on professional advice and documents that appear genuine.

4.5 Indemnity: The Trustee is entitled to be indemnified out of Trust assets for liabilities properly incurred in good faith, provided it has not acted fraudulently, dishonestly, or with gross negligence.

⸻

5. Investment Manager

5.1 The Trustee may appoint NotCentralised Pty Ltd (or replacement) as Investment Manager under a written Investment Management Agreement (IMA).

5.2 The Investment Manager acts as agent of the Trustee and may perform delegated functions including asset origination, portfolio management, smart-contract execution, and reporting.

5.3 The Manager must act in accordance with:
	•	the IMA, this Trust Deed, and the Information Memorandum; and
	•	applicable laws, AFSL conditions (if any), and Trustee directives.

⸻

6. Beneficial Interests and Units

6.1 Units: The beneficial interests in the Trust are divided into Units, each conferring proportionate rights to income and capital.

6.2 Classes: The Trustee may create multiple classes of Units (including sub-trusts) with differing rights or terms, subject to registration on the Platform.

6.3 Issue and Redemption:
(a) Units may be issued, transferred, or redeemed in accordance with the Subscription Agreement and this Deed.
(b) Issue and redemption occur upon blockchain confirmation and Trustee authorisation.

6.4 Register:
	•	The on-chain register maintained by the Platform is the canonical record of ownership.
	•	An off-chain mirror is maintained for reconciliation and regulatory compliance.
	•	In case of inconsistency, the on-chain record prevails after Trustee verification.

6.5 Voting and Governance:
	•	Unitholders may exercise voting rights electronically or via verified wallet signatures.
	•	“Majority Investors” means holders of more than 50% of issued Units of a class.
	•	Resolutions may be conducted on-chain using snapshot block verification.

⸻

7. Income and Distributions

7.1 Trust Income: Income includes all interest, discount yield, and other receipts derived from the assets of the Trust.

7.2 Distribution Frequency: Income distributions are made monthly (or as determined by the Trustee) in cash or as additional Units.

7.3 Unitisation of Yield: The Trustee may distribute additional Units to investors instead of cash to reflect compounding returns.

7.4 Waterfall: All collections and recoveries are applied in the following order:
	1.	Trustee fees and expenses;
	2.	Investment management fees;
	3.	Servicing fees;
	4.	Reserve or credit-enhancement top-ups;
	5.	Distribution to Unitholders pro rata.

7.5 Unpaid Amounts: Undistributed income may be carried forward or allocated as capital at Trustee discretion.

⸻

8. Permitted Investments

The Trustee may invest Trust assets only in:
	•	trade receivables and receivable tokens meeting eligibility criteria;
	•	cash and cash equivalents (including stablecoins fully backed by AUD deposits);
	•	bank deposits with ADIs;
	•	other instruments approved by the Trustee and consistent with the Information Memorandum.

Derivative or leveraged investments are prohibited unless expressly approved by Majority Investors.

⸻

9. Valuation and Unit Pricing

9.1 The Investment Manager must calculate the Net Asset Value (NAV) monthly, verified by the Trustee.

9.2 Unit Price = (NAV ÷ total Units on issue).

9.3 Adjustments must account for accrued income, realised losses, fees, and recoveries.

9.4 Valuation methodology must be consistent with accounting standards and disclosed in the Information Memorandum.

⸻

10. Accounts and Audit

10.1 The Trustee must maintain proper books of account.
10.2 The Trust’s financial year ends on 30 June (unless otherwise agreed).
10.3 Annual financial statements must be prepared in accordance with Australian Accounting Standards and audited by an independent auditor.
10.4 Audit reports must be made available to Unitholders within 90 days of year-end.

⸻

11. Fees and Expenses

The Trustee is entitled to be paid, out of Trust assets:
	•	Trustee Fee;
	•	Investment Management Fee;
	•	Servicing Fee;
	•	Audit, legal, and administration costs; and
	•	all other properly incurred expenses.

All fees are disclosed in the Information Memorandum and Investment Management Agreement.

⸻

12. Meetings and Resolutions

12.1 Convening Meetings: The Trustee may call a meeting of Unitholders at any time, or upon request by holders of at least 20% of Units.

12.2 Quorum: Two or more Unitholders representing at least 25% of issued Units.

12.3 Voting: One vote per Unit. Voting may be conducted electronically via the Platform.

12.4 Special Resolutions: Required for amendments to this Deed, replacement of Trustee, or early termination (≥ 75% of Units by value).

⸻

13. Trustee’s Liability and Indemnity

13.1 The Trustee is not personally liable for any debt or obligation of the Trust beyond the extent of Trust assets, except for fraud, wilful misconduct, or gross negligence.

13.2 The Trustee is indemnified out of the Trust assets for all liabilities properly incurred in connection with its duties.

13.3 Unitholders’ rights are limited to Trust assets; they have no right to claim against the Trustee personally.

⸻

14. Replacement of Trustee

14.1 The Trustee may resign by giving 60 days’ written notice to Unitholders.

14.2 Majority Investors may remove the Trustee by Special Resolution, provided a qualified replacement trustee is simultaneously appointed.

14.3 Upon replacement, the outgoing Trustee must transfer all assets, records, and access credentials to the incoming Trustee.

⸻

15. Records, Audit, and Transparency

15.1 The Trustee must ensure that the Platform maintains immutable records of all token transactions and distributions.

15.2 Investors must have access via secure API or dashboard to performance and distribution data.

15.3 Records shall be retained for at least seven years and subject to periodic external review.

⸻

16. Forks, Migrations, and Manual Settlement

16.1 In the event of a network fork, the Trustee shall designate the canonical blockchain for continuity of the Trust Register.

16.2 If a platform migration occurs, the Trustee shall authorise a snapshot and re-issuance of tokens on the successor platform.

16.3 Should the digital settlement layer fail, the Trustee shall implement manual settlement procedures via off-chain reconciliation and verified records.

⸻

17. Termination

17.1 The Trust terminates upon:
(a) resolution by Special Resolution of Unitholders;
(b) redemption of all Units; or
(c) direction of the Trustee where continuation is impractical or unlawful.

17.2 On termination:
	•	the Trustee must realise Trust assets;
	•	discharge all liabilities; and
	•	distribute the balance to Unitholders pro rata.

17.3 Upon final distribution, all tokens shall be burned or rendered inactive on the Platform.

⸻

18. Confidentiality and Privacy

18.1 The Trustee, Manager, and service providers must maintain confidentiality of all investor and transaction data.

18.2 Personal data shall be handled in accordance with the Privacy Notice and Australian Privacy Principles.

18.3 Immutable ledger data shall be pseudonymised where feasible to protect privacy.

⸻

19. Taxation

19.1 The Trust is intended to be a flow-through vehicle for tax purposes.
19.2 The Trustee may attribute income and capital gains to Unitholders under AMIT principles (if applicable).
19.3 The Trustee may withhold tax as required by law.

⸻

20. Amendments

20.1 The Trustee may amend this Deed with approval of a Special Resolution of Unitholders or as required by law or regulator.

20.2 Minor or administrative amendments may be made without Unitholder consent where they do not adversely affect rights.

20.3 All amendments must be recorded on-chain and timestamped for transparency.

⸻

21. Notices

21.1 Notices may be delivered by:
	•	email;
	•	Platform notification; or
	•	blockchain message to registered wallet.

21.2 Notice is deemed received on confirmation of blockchain transaction or next Business Day following email transmission.

⸻

22. Governing Law

This Deed is governed by the laws of New South Wales, Australia.
