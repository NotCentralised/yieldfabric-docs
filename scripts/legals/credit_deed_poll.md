4. Commitment and Drawdown

4.1 Commitment: The Lender agrees to make available to the Borrower the Facility up to the Facility Limit, subject to satisfaction of all Conditions Precedent in Schedule 1.

4.2 Drawdown Requests:
(a) Must be submitted via the Platform or written notice to the Lender at least one Business Day before the proposed drawdown date.
(b) Must specify the amount, currency, and wallet or account for disbursement.
(c) Are deemed binding once digitally signed or confirmed on-chain by authorised keys of the Trustee.

4.3 Drawdown Mechanics:
	•	Fiat loans shall be paid to the Borrower’s nominated Collections Account.
	•	Tokenised loans shall be disbursed by minting or transferring stablecoins to the Borrower’s whitelisted wallet, evidenced by on-chain hash linked to this Deed.

4.4 Multiple Drawdowns: Permitted within the Availability Period provided the total does not exceed the Facility Limit.

⸻

5. Interest and Fees

5.1 Interest Accrual:
	•	Accrues daily on drawn amounts from the drawdown date until repayment.
	•	Payable monthly in arrears or at maturity.

5.2 Payment Method:
	•	Paid in fiat or stablecoin as specified in the Drawdown Notice.
	•	Interest payments are final once recorded on the blockchain (settlement finality).

5.3 Fees:
	•	Commitment Fee on undrawn balance;
	•	Prepayment Fee if repaid before minimum tenor;
	•	Default Interest on overdue amounts;
	•	Platform Transaction Fees for gas and operational costs.

⸻

6. Repayment and Prepayment

6.1 Scheduled Repayment:
The Borrower must repay each drawdown on its Repayment Date or earlier if accelerated.

6.2 Voluntary Prepayment:
Allowed with 5 Business Days’ notice; accrued interest and fees must be paid concurrently.

6.3 Mandatory Prepayment:
Upon occurrence of an Event of Default, breach of Concentration Limits, or receipt of excess cash collections exceeding permitted reserves.

6.4 Application of Repayments:
Applied in the following order unless the Lender directs otherwise:
	1.	Fees and expenses;
	2.	Accrued interest;
	3.	Principal outstanding;
	4.	Other amounts due.

⸻

7. Security and Perfection

7.1 The Borrower grants to the Lender a first-ranking security interest over the collateral described in the Collateral Security Deed, including:
	•	receivable tokens and proceeds;
	•	collections accounts and fiat balances;
	•	stablecoin wallets under Trustee control.

7.2 The Lender shall perfect its security by registration on the PPSR and by control over digital wallets and accounts.

7.3 The Security Trustee holds the Security on trust for the Lender and other secured parties.

⸻

8. Conditions Precedent

Before the first drawdown, the Borrower must deliver:
	•	certified copy of the Trust Deed;
	•	executed Investment Management Agreement;
	•	executed Collateral Security Deed;
	•	legal opinion confirming enforceability and true-sale status;
	•	evidence of PPSR registrations;
	•	board or Trustee resolutions;
	•	KYC and sanctions clearances; and
	•	operational readiness confirmation from the Platform.

No funds will be advanced until all are satisfied to the Lender’s satisfaction.

The Borrower represents that:
	1.	It is duly established and has full capacity to enter this Deed.
	2.	The Trust is validly constituted and compliant with all laws.
	3.	All information provided is true, complete, and not misleading.
	4.	All necessary authorisations have been obtained.
	5.	It has not incurred any other indebtedness or security conflicting with this Facility.

Each representation is deemed repeated on each drawdown and interest-payment date.

10. Undertakings

The Borrower covenants that it will:
	•	maintain existence of the Trust and comply with all laws;
	•	promptly pay all sums due under this Deed;
	•	ensure all receivable and collateral data remain accurate and verifiable;
	•	maintain AML/CTF compliance and cooperate with audits;
	•	notify the Lender of any default or regulatory investigation;
	•	maintain insurance over receivables and systems;
	•	preserve perfection of all security interests;
	•	not create or permit other security without Lender consent;
	•	not materially change platform governance or smart-contract logic without prior written notice.

⸻

11. Events of Default

Events of Default include:
	1.	Non-payment of any amount due under this Deed.
	2.	Breach of covenant or warranty not remedied within 5 Business Days.
	3.	Insolvency, external administration, or dissolution of the Borrower or Trustee.
	4.	Unauthorised transfer, duplication, or loss of Receivable Tokens.
	5.	Default under the Collateral Security Deed or Investment Management Agreement.
	6.	Misrepresentation or falsified data on the Platform.
	7.	Regulatory or AML/CTF breach or sanctions listing.
	8.	Material adverse change or system failure impairing obligations.

Upon default, the Lender may (without notice):
	•	suspend further drawdowns;
	•	demand immediate repayment;
	•	enforce security;
	•	exercise set-off rights against any related trust balances; and
	•	assume operational control of the collateral wallets.

⸻

12. Enforcement and Application of Proceeds

12.1 The Lender or Security Trustee may enforce security by:
	•	sale of collateral receivables (on-chain or private sale);
	•	appropriation of stablecoin balances;
	•	appointment of a receiver or replacement trustee.

12.2 Waterfall of Enforcement Proceeds:
	1.	Costs of enforcement;
	2.	Lender principal, interest, and fees;
	3.	Residual balance to Borrower’s Trust Account.

12.3 Settlement events are verified via blockchain transaction hashes linked to enforcement reports.

⸻

13. Set-Off and Netting

The Lender may set off any amount due by the Borrower under this Deed against any amount payable by the Lender (or any other Acacia Trust) to the Borrower.

⸻

14. Notices

14.1 Notices may be delivered by:

	•	secure Platform message; or
	•	blockchain transaction (signed wallet message).

14.2 Notice is deemed received upon blockchain confirmation or next Business Day after email transmission.
